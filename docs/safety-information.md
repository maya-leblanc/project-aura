# safety-information

Safety information is sectionally divided below.

1)	Battery Safety Information:
•	Do not drop or subject to impact – risk of explosion or fire.
•	Keep away from open flames, sparks, or temperatures exceeding manufacturer specifications.
•	Do not expose terminals; avoid metal tools touching battery poles.
•	Follow local hazardous waste disposal procedures; never throw in regular trash.
•	Use only manufacturer-approved chargers; overcharging can cause overheating or swelling.

2)	Computer Engineering Components – Basic Safety:
•	Use an anti-static wrist strap when handling circuit boards or sensitive ICs.
•	Ensure systems are unplugged before inserting/removing components (RAM, GPUs, etc.).
•	Ensure active cooling systems (fans/heat sinks) are installed and functional to prevent overheating.
•	Avoid forcing connectors; double-check alignment and pin orientation.
•	Keep away from liquids, extreme temperatures, and dust buildup.

3)	General Warnings:
•	Applies to batteries, PCBs, and headsets – impact can cause functional or safety failure.
•	Especially important for lithium-based batteries and plastic casings – can release toxic fumes or explode.
•	Mark sensitive components and batteries with handling instructions and hazard symbols.

4)	EEG Headset Safety Information:
•	Clean headset sensors before and after use to prevent skin irritation or infection.
•	Only use devices certified for medical or consumer EEG applications – never tamper with internal electronics.
•	Do not wear for extended periods (typically <60 mins per session unless otherwise advised).
•	Avoid use near water, strong magnetic fields, or high-voltage equipment.
•	Not suitable for individuals with implanted medical devices (e.g., pacemakers) unless cleared by a doctor.
