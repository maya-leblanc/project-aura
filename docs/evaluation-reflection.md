# evaluation-reflection

I tested how accurately the EEG headset responded to the mental commands I trained using Emotiv BCI. I used “LIFT” to open the robotic hand and “DROP” to close it. The system worked best when I was completely focused and calm, any distractions or signs of mental fatigue threw it off. There were times the prosthetic didn’t respond right away, which made it clear how important signal strength and sensor placement are. I had to adjust the headset a few times, and each small tweak noticeably improved responsiveness.

Wearing the headset and prosthetic for longer periods also made me think more about comfort and practicality. It’s one thing to get it working in short bursts, but sustained use adds another layer of challenge. This whole process made me realize just how sensitive and personal brain-computer interfaces are. Even something as abstract as a thought needs to be trained, refined, and treated like a signal. It also made me think about ways to improve the system, maybe adding more distinct mental commands or reducing the delay in response. Seeing the hand move with just my thoughts was honestly surreal, it made the hours of troubleshooting feel worth it.
